I am a PhD Candidate in Political Science at the University of Rochester.
My fields of interest are Comparative Politics and Political Methodology. My researh focuses on democratic political institutions in non-demicratic environment, regional and local development, and public goods provision with an implication of causal inference tools. I work with both historical and contemporary data. My primary regional focus is Russia and Eastern Europe.

My dissertation explores political interactions between regional and local elites; the incentives of regional and local officials to choose between upward and downward accountability in the non-demicratic regimes, and the effects of such choices on the regional and local development.

Another project looks at the regional and local elites through the perspective of various survival mechanisms. It indentifies career concerns and the consequent political strategies of the regional political elites in the non-democratic regimes.

My other project I look at the current local development and public goods provision in India as long-term effects of colonial institutions.

I have conducted field work in Russia, including archival work in State Archive of the Russian Federation and Russian State Historical Archive. I also created a guide for archival work in the Russian state archives.
